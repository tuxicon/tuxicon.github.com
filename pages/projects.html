TurkBench

Benjamin V. Hanrahan, Jutta K. Willamowski, Saiganesh Swaminathan, David B. Martin In Proceedings of CHI '15 (Note, to appear).

Crowdworkers face a numerous challenges in turking or working in online labor markerts. They lack the tools and ways to navigate the market efficiently. We built a tool TurkBench that provides workers with a personalized market visualization and session management(more information coming soon)

MakerVis

Saiganesh Swaminathan, Conglei Shi, Yvonne Jansen, Pierre Dragicevic, Lora Oehlberg, Jean-Daniel Fekete. In Proceedings of CHI '14 .

An increasing variety of physical visualizations are being built. However, crafting them remains a laborious process and demands expertise in both data visualization and digital fabrication. We present the MakerVis prototype, the first tool that integrates the whole workflow, from data filtering to physical fabrication.

Emojah

Silver Sepp, Saiganesh Swaminathan, Tamas Ling, jelena Pantovic and Alex Van De Merwe. TechCrunch Disrupt Hackathon @ Berlin 2013.

We hacked on a tool that color codes chat messages based on emotions detected using front facing camera.

Tweetzoom

Ye Lin and Saiganesh Swaminathan. Course work for HCID 101 - Introduction to HCI.

Inspired by the PolyZoom interaction technique, We developed an application to explore twitter topics across several locations simultaneously by leveraging the multiscale and multi-focus technniques of PolyZoom to explore 2D visual spaces

Floorcom

Saiganesh Swaminathan, Luis Fernando Colin, and Shailful Alam. Course work for HCID 102 - User-Centered Design Methods.

Conferences present a huge opportunity for networking, collaborating, attending interesting talks, etc. At the same time performing those activities simultaneously involves several challenges. We interviewed several researchers, sketched storyboards, design concepts and finally video-prototyped a interactive floor called "Floorcom"

HViz Garden

Saiganesh Swaminathan, Luis Fernando Colin, and Shailful Alam. Course work for HCID 201 - Design project.

Planning and keeping progress of health is a difficult task, users are faced with several challenges such as maintaining health routines and habits. We present an interactive tool for visualizing health activities across different sports in a playful manner.

PIMTouch

Saiganesh Swaminathan. Bachelor Thesis, ETH Zürich, Feb 2012.

Understanding personal information management practices is very critical to design systems that help people to manage their information digitally. We studied how researchers collect, organize, manage and use physical tools in real world for managing publications. Responding to the unique design constraints, we designed PIMTouch an interactive tool to manage research activites in multi-touch screen contexts.

Flickrtouch

Saiganesh Swaminathan. Research Intern, ETH Zürich, Sep 2011.

Web interfaces need to be adapted to be usable for touch interactions. We designed a prototype FlickrTouch which consisted of an adapted a touch interface to enable rich interactions.
